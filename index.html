A read on where blockchain actually fits in eGreen's stack, and where it stops fitting.

You came out of Techstars Paris '22 with a token thesis that was defensible at the time. Three years on, Patagonia, GEDI, and 3Mt of offtake tell a different story. dMRV, science, and integrity. This is a one-page look at that gap, and where a short engagement would move the needle.

The hypothesis

The jacket is costing you the contract.

Your offtake conversations stall because the buyer's compliance team still sees "EGRN COIN" in your materials and stops reading before they get to the dMRV story. The crypto-carbon reset of 2023 did not kill the tokenization idea. It did kill the buyer's patience for hearing about it first.

Meanwhile the real asset is already sitting in your stack. Sensor fusion, satellite imagery processing, uncertainty quantification on tonnage. That is where the science lives and where the price premium gets earned. A CSO should be able to walk from a contract line to the exact model run and sensor timestamp that produced the credit. That walk is an orchestration problem.

Your offtake story is a science story wearing a blockchain jacket, and the jacket is what is costing you the big corporate contracts.
A frame for the stack

60 · 30 · 10. Read each layer honestly.

A simple way to look at what moves a credit from a Patagonian easement to a corporate buyer's ledger. Price the AI layer like the science it is. Build the rest with the cheapest thing that will hold up under audit.

Traditional
60%

Code and database work

Project registry, audit logs, reconciliation with Verra or Gold Standard, the portal a buyer signs through. Postgres and a good data model carry this layer. An LLM does not belong here.

Rule-based
30%

Issuance and governance logic

ICROA alignment checks, offtake allocation, retirement triggers, standards conformance. Deterministic rules. Explainable. Auditable by a compliance team without a data scientist in the room.

Genuinely AI
10%

Sensor and satellite science

GEDI ingest, sensor fusion, uncertainty bounds on tonnage, reconciliation with ground truth. The 10 percent that earns the premium and writes the audit trail that closes the offtake.

The paper behind the method
Interpretable Context Methodology (ICM)

Folder structure as agent architecture.

Submitted to ACM TiiS. MIT-licensed. Layered context from L0 identity through L4 working artifacts, with measurable interpretability and reproducibility gains. Applied to eGreen, it is a way to structure the dMRV-to-credit context so a buyer's auditor can trace any claim back to source. Raw imagery, sensor reads, project metadata, scientific validation, credit issuance record. One path from pixel to ledger.
